GAP Service (Garage Automation Panel)

GAP is designed with over-the-counter parts so repairs are fast and affordable. This page covers GAP-specific troubleshooting, parts, and swap steps.

What to send Support (fastest resolution)

  • Unit Label: YYWW + last 4 of MAC (example: 2549-7E6D)
  • Inside-floor enclosure version mark: example GAP_BOX_v7.3
  • Photo of wiring (wide shot + close of power + relay)
  • Symptom summary: “reboots on relay”, “door state inverted”, “eth flaps”, etc.

Quick Triage (GAP)

  1. Power first: most “random” issues are brownouts or loose DC wiring.
  2. Check grounds: all grounds must be common (buck(s), relay, ESP32, sensors).
  3. Relay click test: does the relay LED/click occur when commanded?
  4. Swap test: swap a suspect reed/PIR with a known-good part if available.

Common Symptoms → Likely Cause

Symptom Most likely causes Fix
Reboots when relay toggles Undersized buck, loose DC input, ground issue Verify buck headroom, tighten DC wiring, keep grounds common, shorten power leads.
Door shows open when closed Reed type mismatch (NO/NC), magnet alignment Confirm reed type and wiring, align magnet, invert input in firmware only if needed.
Ethernet flaps 3.3V sag, cable/switch port, LAN8720 wiring Confirm stable 3.3V, try different cable/port, verify LAN8720 pin map used by your baseline.
PIR false triggers Noise, vibration, sensitivity too high Reduce sensitivity, improve mounting stability, verify power stability and routing.

Swap Steps (FRU style)

Swap a buck converter (fastest “random reboot” fix)
  1. Power off and disconnect DC supply.
  2. Label input/output wires before removing.
  3. Install replacement buck, verify polarity, then power on.
  4. Verify stable output voltage before reconnecting loads.
Swap relay module
  1. Power off.
  2. Move one wire at a time to the new relay module.
  3. Power on and test relay toggle.
Swap ESP32 board
  1. Power off.
  2. Replace the board (same model family).
  3. Flash the correct baseline firmware / restore backup.
  4. Confirm network + sensor readings.
